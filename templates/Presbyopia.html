Presbyopia

Presbyopia is an age-related condition where the eye gradually loses its ability to focus on close objects. It typically starts to become noticeable in the early to mid-40s and worsens over time. It occurs because the lens inside the eye becomes less flexible, making it harder to focus light directly onto the retina.

Symptoms:

  • Difficulty reading small print, especially in dim light
  • Needing to hold reading material farther away
  • Eye strain or headaches during near tasks

Treatment:

Presbyopia can be corrected with reading glasses, bifocals, progressive lenses, multifocal contact lenses, or surgical options such as lens implants.
