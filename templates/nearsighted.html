Nearsightedness (Myopia)

Myopia, or nearsightedness, is a refractive error that makes far-away objects look blurry, but nearby objects are clear. but nearby objects are clear. This happens when the shape of the eye causes light to focus in front of the retina instead of on it. This occurs when either the eyeball grows too long from front to back or the cornea of the eye is curved too steeply. These problems can impact anyone, but it typically develops during childhood or adolescence. You are also more at risk of developing myopia if your family members have it.

This happens when the shape of your eye causes light rays to bend incorrectly, focusing images in front of your retina instead of on it.

Symptoms:

  • Blurry vision when looking at distant objects
  • Squinting or partially closing the eyelids to see clearly
  • Eye strain and headaches

Treatment:

Myopia can usually be corrected with prescription eyeglasses, contact lenses, or refractive surgery such as LASIK.
