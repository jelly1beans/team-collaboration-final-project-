Nearsightedness (Myopia)

Nearsightedness is when close objects appear clear, but distant objects look blurry. It occurs when the eye’s shape causes light rays to bend incorrectly, focusing in front of the retina.

×

Farsightedness (Hyperopia)

Farsightedness is when distant objects may be seen more clearly than nearby ones. It occurs when light focuses behind the retina instead of on it.

×

Astigmatism

Astigmatism happens when the cornea or lens is irregularly shaped, causing blurred or distorted vision.

×

Presbyopia

Presbyopia is an age-related condition where the eye gradually loses the ability to focus on nearby objects.

×

Cataracts

Cataracts occur when the eye’s lens becomes cloudy, leading to blurry vision and, if untreated, vision loss.
