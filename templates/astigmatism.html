Astigmatism

Astigmatism is a refractive error caused by an irregular shape of the cornea or lens, which prevents light from focusing evenly on the retina. Instead of a single clear focus point, vision appears blurred or distorted at all distances.

Symptoms:

  • Blurry or distorted vision at all distances
  • Eye strain or discomfort
  • Difficulty seeing at night
  • Squinting to improve focus

Treatment:

Astigmatism can usually be corrected with prescription eyeglasses, contact lenses (including toric lenses), or refractive surgery.
