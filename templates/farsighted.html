Farsightedness (Hyperopia)

Hyperopia, also known as farsightedness or hypermetropia, is a refractive error that makes nearby objects look blurry. This happens when the eyeball grows too short from front to back or the cornea is too flat. As a result, light gets focused behind the retina rather than on it. Most people who are farsighted are born with it, but may not encounter any vision problems until they’re older at which reading glasses may be prescribed. In fact, you’re more prone to hyperopia if family members also have it. If left untreated, hyperopia can cause eye strain and headaches.

Symptoms:

  • Blurry vision when looking at close objects
  • Eye strain when reading or doing near work
  • Headaches after prolonged close-up tasks

Treatment:

Farsightedness can be corrected with prescription eyeglasses, contact lenses, or refractive surgery such as LASIK.
